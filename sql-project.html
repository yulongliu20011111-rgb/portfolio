Game Launch ROI Analysis: Seasun Games

Analyzing influencer marketing performance on Douyin (TikTok China) and Rednote (Xiaohongshu) to optimize user acquisition for Kingsoft Seasun Games.

1. The Business Challenge

The goal of this project was to evaluate influencer marketing efficiency across Douyin and Rednote for a game campaign update. However, unlike e-commerce products, game-related content does not always translate directly into measurable immediate purchases, making true ROI attribution significantly more complex.

Key Metrics Calculated

  • Weighted Engagement Score
  • Cost Per Engagement (CPE)
  • Cross-Platform Efficiency

Tech Stack

  • PostgreSQL
  • Window Functions (DENSE_RANK)
  • CTEs (Common Table Expressions)

2. Data Realities: Messy Data & Nuances

  • The Attribution Trap: One key challenge was that flat engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares) do not necessarily reflect actual player acquisition or long-term retention. For example, a high number of "likes" on Douyin may indicate short-term attention, while on Rednote, "saves" and secondary content creation (fan guides, reposts) often better reflect deeper community engagement.
  • Handling Incomplete Data: Engagement logs from the Douyin API contained a 5% null rate due to server timeouts during peak launch hours. I addressed this by applying a rolling 7-day average imputation for missing hourly data to maintain overall aggregate accuracy without distorting the trend.

3. The SQL Execution

PostgreSQL

I used SQL (CTEs + Window Functions) to calculate engagement rates and Cost Per Engagement (CPE) across influencers. However, as the query output revealed, these metrics alone were insufficient to determine true campaign effectiveness in the gaming context. 

-- Step 1: Aggregate engagement metrics per influencer & platform
WITH Engagement_Summary AS (
    SELECT 
        i.influencer_name,
        i.content_category,
        i.platform,
        c.budget_allocation,
        SUM(p.views) AS total_views,
        SUM(p.likes + p.comments + p.shares) AS total_engagements
    FROM influencers i
    JOIN posts p ON i.influencer_id = p.influencer_id
    JOIN campaigns c ON p.campaign_id = c.campaign_id
    WHERE c.campaign_name = 'Seasun_Q3_Expansion'
    GROUP BY 1, 2, 3, 4
),

-- Step 2: Calculate KPI ratios (Engagement Rate & CPE)
Performance_Calc AS (
    SELECT
        *,
        ROUND((total_engagements::numeric / NULLIF(total_views,0)) * 100, 2) AS engagement_rate,
        ROUND(budget_allocation / NULLIF(total_engagements,0), 2) AS cost_per_engagement
    FROM Engagement_Summary
)

-- Step 3: Rank top performers by Platform efficiency
SELECT 
    influencer_name,
    content_category,
    platform,
    engagement_rate AS "Eng_Rate_Pct",
    cost_per_engagement AS "CPE_RMB",
    DENSE_RANK() OVER (
        PARTITION BY platform 
        ORDER BY cost_per_engagement ASC
    ) AS rank_in_platform
FROM Performance_Calc
WHERE total_views > 100000 -- Filter for meaningful campaign reach
ORDER BY platform, rank_in_platform 
LIMIT 5;

The Analytical Limitation

The analysis revealed a critical limitation: traditional performance marketing metrics do not fully capture the value of community-driven content. In particular, Rednote creators who generated secondary content (e.g. gameplay guides, reposts) appeared to have a stronger long-term impact on player retention, even if their short-term CPE was not the lowest on the board.

5. Business Recommendations

Instead of directly optimizing purely for the lowest Cost Per Engagement (CPE), I suggested a more nuanced approach for future gaming campaigns:

  • Combine Metrics: Pair quantitative engagement metrics with qualitative indicators (e.g., content format, depth of lore discussion, repost behavior) to assess true value.
  • Prioritize Catalysts: Focus budget on creators who act as "community seeds"—those who actively trigger secondary content creation and UGC (User Generated Content) rather than just passive viewing.
  • Develop Alternative KPIs: Establish custom tracking metrics for game campaigns, such as "Cost Per Save" or "Guide Completion Rate", moving beyond standard social media vanity metrics.

Analyst Reflection

This project showed that not all marketing performance can be reduced to clean numerical metrics. While SQL is excellent for extracting baseline efficiency rates, a good analyst must recognize when the data model fails to capture the complexity of human behavior—especially within highly engaged gaming communities.
